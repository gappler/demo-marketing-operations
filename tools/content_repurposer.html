Five Days in the Wind River Range with the Basecamp 45L

The Wind River Range doesn't care what you're carrying. Granite Pass doesn't get easier because your pack cost more. Island Lake doesn't look different through the mesh of a premium hip belt.

But the pack on your back determines whether you're thinking about the trail or thinking about your shoulders. And after five days on the Elkhart Park loop, the thing I noticed most about the Basecamp 45L was how little I noticed it.

Day one. 38 lbs at the trailhead. Food for five days, a bear canister wedged vertically against the framesheet, and one too many layers because I still don't trust July weather above 10,000 feet. The Basecamp's load lifters pulled the weight high and centered. The hip belt did what hip belts are supposed to do — transferred 80% of the load to my hips so my shoulders could just stabilize.

Day three. Half the food was gone, which usually means a sloppy pack that's lost its frame geometry. The Basecamp compressed down cleanly. The thermoformed HDPE framesheet doesn't rely on a full load to hold its shape — it's rigid enough to carry well at 25 lbs or 38.

Day five. Back at the truck. The 1680D sailcloth nylon had granite dust ground into every surface. The YKK AquaGuard zippers were full of trail grit from an afternoon thunderstorm on day four. Everything still worked.

I've carried enough packs to have opinions about all of them. Most of those opinions are complaints. The Basecamp didn't give me anything to complain about. It carried the weight, handled the weather, compressed when it needed to, and disappeared into the trip.

That's what good gear does. It stops being gear and starts being invisible. The Basecamp 45L does that better than anything I've carried in twenty years.

Yowie Basecamp 45L. $385. Lifetime warranty. No receipt required.
