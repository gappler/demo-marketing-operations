# Basecamp 45L — Email Campaign Sequence

## Sequence Strategy

Five emails over 30 days to a list of 4,200 subscribers. The audience is experienced outdoor adults aged 40–65 who are skeptical of marketing, read specs before copy, and buy based on materials and construction — not hype.

The sequence follows a trust arc, not a pressure arc:

1. **Announce** — say what it is, plainly
2. **Prove** — show what it's made of
3. **Contextualize** — put it in a real scenario
4. **De-risk** — address the $385 hesitation
5. **Close** — one last word, then silence

Every email respects the reader's intelligence. No urgency tricks, no countdown timers, no "limited stock" language. This audience sees through it, and it damages trust.

## Voice Guidelines (Applied)

- Short sentences. Short paragraphs. Whitespace is a feature.
- No exclamation marks. Ever.
- No "we're excited" or "we're thrilled." State facts.
- The reader has carried a pack before. Don't explain what a framesheet is.
- CTAs are direct and low-pressure: "See the pack." Not "Shop now" or "Don't miss out."

**Timing rationale:**
- 7:00 AM local targets the pre-work window when this demographic checks email
- Weekend send on Email 4 (Saturday) catches a more reflective reading state — appropriate for a consideration-stage message
- 4–7 day gaps between emails prevent fatigue on a small list
- Sequence ends Day 27, leaving 3 days of silence before the campaign closes — no frantic last-day blast
